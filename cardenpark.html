Carden Park Hotel

Commercial M&E Package
Project Value £TBC
Hospitality  ·  Cheshire
Executive Summary

Carden Park Hotel & Spa, a luxury retreat in Cheshire, offers premium spa facilities, fine dining and top-tier accommodation. To reduce energy costs and improve efficiency, the hotel chose Vital Synergy to upgrade its outdated heating and ventilation systems. The new solution modernises infrastructure, supports sustainability goals and ensures consistent comfort for guests and staff.

Scope of Works
Ducting & Supply Infrastructure

New 400mm spiral ducting and 200mm banner supply ducts installed throughout the facility. Custom bracketry designed and fabricated to suit the specific structural constraints of the hotel, ensuring a precise and secure installation across all areas.

VRF Systems & Energy Controls

Modern Variable Refrigerant Flow (VRF) systems installed alongside advanced energy controls and enhanced insulation. Exposed air-handling units fitted throughout, delivering improved climate management and a significant reduction in operational energy consumption.

Commissioning & Documentation

Comprehensive commissioning activities completed including airflow checks, system balancing and verification procedures. Detailed documentation of all installation work prepared to ensure long-term performance, traceability and full compliance with current regulations.

Compliance & Test Points

Dedicated test points installed at all key locations across the system to facilitate regulatory inspections and ongoing compliance monitoring. All works carried out in line with current industry standards, with inspection access designed in from the outset.

Project Overview

Project Detail

Carden Park Hotel & Spa appointed Vital Synergy to deliver a full commercial M&E upgrade of its heating and ventilation systems. The existing infrastructure had reached the end of its serviceable life and was contributing to elevated energy costs and inconsistent climate control across the hotel and spa. The scope of works covered new ductwork, VRF systems, energy controls, commissioning and supporting documentation.

The project was carried out with minimal disruption to hotel operations, with phased working arrangements agreed with the client to protect the guest experience throughout the installation programme.

Ducting & Supply Infrastructure

New 400mm spiral ducting was installed to form the primary distribution network, with 200mm banner supply ducts providing coverage to individual zones across the building. All ductwork supports were fabricated using custom bracketry designed specifically for the facility, accounting for the structural character of the existing building fabric and ensuring clean, secure routing with minimal visual impact.

Duct runs were coordinated with other building services and the principal contractor to avoid clashes and allow concurrent works to proceed without delay. All new ductwork was installed in accordance with current HVCA standards and pressure tested on completion.

VRF Systems & Energy Controls

Modern Variable Refrigerant Flow systems were selected and installed to replace the legacy heating and cooling equipment. VRF technology allows individual zones to be controlled independently, delivering precise temperatures across guest rooms, spa areas and dining spaces without the energy waste associated with fixed-output systems. Advanced energy controls were integrated to provide centralised monitoring and allow the hotel team to manage consumption across the building from a single interface.

Enhanced insulation was applied to all new pipe and duct runs, and exposed air-handling units were installed in service areas. The combination of VRF technology and improved insulation performance has delivered a marked reduction in the hotel's heating and cooling energy demand.

Commissioning & Handover

All systems were subject to a full commissioning programme on completion of the installation phase. Airflow checks and system balancing were carried out across all zones, with results verified against the design specification. Verification procedures confirmed that all installed equipment was operating within the intended parameters prior to handover.

Detailed documentation was compiled throughout the project and issued to the client at practical completion. The handover package included as-installed drawings, commissioning records, equipment data sheets and maintenance schedules, providing the hotel with a complete reference for the ongoing management of the new systems.

Key Benefits Delivered

  • Reduced energy consumption. Lower carbon footprint across heating and ventilation, aligned with the hotel's sustainability targets and industry-wide decarbonisation goals.
  • Lower operational costs. A notable reduction in heating and cooling expenditure has been achieved through the combination of VRF technology, advanced controls and improved insulation.
  • Enhanced guest comfort. Precise climate control, improved air circulation and consistent conditions across the hotel and spa deliver a noticeably better environment for guests and staff alike.
