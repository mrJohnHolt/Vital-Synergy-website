Case Study / Hospitality

Carden Park Hotel and Spa, Cheshire

Mechanical and Electrical Upgrade
Project Value £1.5m
2024 – 2025
Project Overview

Vital Synergy delivered a comprehensive mechanical and electrical upgrade at Carden Park Hotel and Spa, a luxury estate in Cheshire, replacing outdated heating and ventilation infrastructure with modern systems designed to reduce energy consumption and improve internal environmental conditions. The installation was completed as a coordinated programme with minimal disruption to the hotel's ongoing operations, and included full commissioning, system documentation and handover to the client.

Client and Sector

Client Overview

Client Carden Park Hotel and Spa, Cheshire

Carden Park Hotel and Spa is a luxury hotel and estate in Cheshire offering spa facilities, fine dining and accommodation across the property. The client required a full upgrade of the building's heating and ventilation systems to reduce energy consumption, lower operating costs and improve the comfort of guests and staff across all areas of the hotel.

Sector Hospitality

Delivering building services works within a live luxury hotel environment required careful planning to protect the guest experience throughout the programme. System upgrades were sequenced to minimise noise and disruption, and all installation activities were coordinated to avoid impact on occupied areas and the hotel's day-to-day operations.

Scope of Works
Ductwork and Distribution

New 400 millimetre spiral ductwork and 200 millimetre burner supply ducts were installed throughout the hotel. Custom bracketry was fabricated and fitted to suit the building layout, supporting improved airflow distribution across all serviced areas of the estate.

HVAC Systems and Controls

Modern variable refrigerant flow systems were installed alongside advanced energy control systems, replacing the existing legacy equipment. These upgrades delivered improved control of heating and cooling across all occupied hotel and spa spaces, with zone-level precision not possible with the previous installation.

Air Handling and Insulation

New air handling units were installed as part of the upgrade programme. Insulation was enhanced across all upgraded systems to improve air quality, reduce heat loss and maximise the efficiency gains delivered by the new plant and controls infrastructure.

Inspection and Compliance

Test points were installed throughout the upgraded systems to support ongoing inspection, testing and regulatory compliance. These provisions allow system performance to be verified during normal operation and provide efficient access for future maintenance and inspection activities.

Results

Key Outcomes and Benefits

Reduced Energy Consumption

The upgraded heating and ventilation systems delivered a significant reduction in energy usage compared with the previous installation, supporting the hotel's ongoing energy management objectives.

Lower Operating Costs

Replacement of inefficient legacy equipment resulted in a measurable reduction in heating and cooling costs, providing a direct and ongoing benefit to the hotel's operational expenditure.

Improved Guest Comfort

Environmental conditions across the hotel and spa were improved following the upgrade. The new systems provide more consistent temperature control and improved air circulation throughout all occupied areas.

Modern VRF Systems Installed

Modern variable refrigerant flow systems replaced legacy equipment, giving the hotel precise zone-level control of heating and cooling with significantly greater efficiency than the previous installation.

Full Commissioning Completed

Comprehensive commissioning was carried out on completion, including airflow checks, system balancing and functional verification confirming all upgraded systems operate in line with design requirements.

Minimal Disruption to Operations

The installation programme was carefully coordinated to protect hotel operations throughout the works. The project was completed as a phased upgrade with no significant impact on guests or hotel services at any stage.

Compliance, Safety and Delivery

Project Detail

What We Delivered

The Carden Park project involved the upgrade and optimisation of key mechanical systems within a large, operational hospitality estate, incorporating the strip out of ageing plant and the installation of modern heating and plantroom infrastructure to improve overall system performance and efficiency. The works included enhancements to the existing distribution systems, upgrades to pipework and insulation, and the integration of new plant and controls to provide a more reliable and energy efficient solution across the site.

The following phase focused on the integration and commissioning of upgraded systems, including refinements to control strategies and the extension of building management system (BMS) functionality to optimise performance across multiple areas. All works were carefully coordinated within a live hotel environment, incorporating full system testing, balancing and commissioning to deliver a stable, efficient and high performing energy solution that supports ongoing operational demands.

Key Benefits

  1. Improved energy efficiency through upgraded plant and optimised system performance.
  2. Enhanced reliability and resilience of heating infrastructure across a large operational estate.
  3. Minimal disruption to hotel operations through carefully coordinated works in a live environment.
  4. Improved system control and performance through integration of advanced BMS strategies.
