Case Study / Hospitality

Hampton by Hilton,
Liverpool John Lennon Airport

Hospitality Plantroom Remediation
Project Value £57,090 ex VAT (core scope)
Tendered Feb 2024, commissioned March 2026
Executive Summary

A live, occupied hotel required remediation following the failure of a pressure reducing valve that caused internal flooding. Vital Synergy delivered a fully phased programme of PRV replacement and plantroom upgrade across the rising shafts and ground floor plantroom, scoped to limit disruption to hotel operation and guests throughout. What began as targeted PRV remediation expanded into a wider booster pump and hot water generation upgrade, restoring resilience and bringing the plantroom infrastructure back to a future-ready standard.

Scope of Works
Core Scope Delivered

Full replacement of pressure reducing valves across the rising shafts, with new full-bore lever isolation valves to support future maintenance. Thermal insulation reinstated to all affected pipework, with system flushing, chlorination and sterilisation completed under controlled conditions.

Booster Set & Pump Upgrade

Existing fixed-speed duty/standby booster set replaced with new variable speed pumps and inverter controls, improving energy efficiency, reducing pressure shock across the distribution and supporting more reliable supply to the upper floors.

Plantroom & Hot Water Generation

Hot water generation upgraded with new thermal stores, plate heat exchanger and supporting pipework infrastructure. New Bolimno two-part valves, Grundfos secondary return pump and full set of cylinder and butterfly isolations installed to bring the plantroom up to a serviceable, future-ready standard.

Handover & Commissioning

Comprehensive RAMS prepared and issued ahead of works to support a live operational environment. Commissioning closed out March 2026 with full as-fitted documentation, mechanical drawings and a complete plantroom commissioning record provided at handover.

Project Overview

Project Detail

The Hampton by Hilton at Liverpool John Lennon Airport presented a complex remediation challenge. A pressure reducing valve failure had caused internal flooding across multiple floors of the occupied hotel, requiring a carefully sequenced response that could be delivered without closing the building or disrupting guests. Vital Synergy was appointed to design and deliver a phased programme of works covering the affected rising shafts and the ground floor plantroom.

The scope was developed following a full survey of the plantroom infrastructure, which identified a number of additional deficiencies beyond the immediate PRV failure. The decision was taken to address these within the same programme, avoiding the need for a return visit and ensuring the hotel would be left with a plantroom capable of reliable long-term operation.

PRV Replacement & Pipework

The core scope centred on the full replacement of pressure reducing valves across the rising shafts. New full-bore lever isolation valves were installed at each location to allow individual sections to be isolated during future maintenance without the need to take down the wider system. All replaced sections were re-insulated with appropriate thermal insulation to reinstate the standard required under current guidance.

On completion of the pipework works, the system was flushed, chlorinated and sterilised in accordance with water hygiene requirements. This was completed under controlled conditions given the operational nature of the building, with careful management of water supply to occupied floors throughout.

Booster Set & Variable Speed Controls

The existing fixed-speed duty/standby booster set was found to be a contributory factor in the pressure shock that had accelerated valve degradation. This was replaced with a new variable speed pump arrangement with inverter controls, providing a more graduated pressure response across the distribution network. The new arrangement improves energy efficiency and reduces wear across the distribution system, delivering more consistent supply pressure to the upper floors.

The inverter controls were configured to the specific demand profile of the building, with set points agreed with the hotel's facilities team prior to commissioning to ensure the system performed correctly under occupancy conditions.

Hot Water Generation & Plantroom Upgrade

The hot water generation system was upgraded as part of the wider plantroom remediation. New thermal stores and a plate heat exchanger were installed, supported by new pipework interconnections and isolation infrastructure. New Bolimno two-part valves were fitted throughout, with a Grundfos secondary return pump installed to maintain temperature across the hot water distribution circuit.

A full complement of cylinder and butterfly isolations was installed to provide the plantroom with the flexibility required for future maintenance. The combined effect of these works brought the plantroom from a reactive maintenance position to a standard that will support reliable operation over the medium to long term.

Commissioning & Handover

Given the live operational environment, comprehensive Risk Assessments and Method Statements were prepared and submitted for approval ahead of each phase of works. All works were sequenced to maintain hot water and cold-water supply to occupied floors at all times, with planned outages agreed and communicated to the hotel management team in advance.

Commissioning was completed in March 2026. Full as-fitted documentation, mechanical drawings and a complete plantroom commissioning record were produced and handed over to the client, providing a clear baseline for ongoing management and future maintenance planning.
