St Peter's Health Centre, Burnley

MEP Refurbishment
Project Value £TBC
August 2025 – March 2026
Executive Summary

A comprehensive multi-package scope encompassing Mechanical, Electrical, Building Management System (BMS) and Public Health (P/H) elements, delivered under the Vital Synergy Subcontract Agreement.

Scope of Works
Mechanical & Electrical Scope

Complete strip-out of redundant services followed by installation of new Low Temperature Hot Water (LTHW) pipework. Isolation valves and Geberit multi-layer pipework included, with phenolic insulation applied to enhance energy efficiency and extend system longevity.

Ventilation & Water Systems

Mechanical Ventilation with Heat Recovery (MVHR) units installed with associated fire dampers for safety. Enhancements to hot and cold-water systems and installation of new sanitaryware throughout the facility.

Electrical & Data Infrastructure

New electrical installations providing updated power supply, containment and integrated BMS connectivity from ninth-floor plant area down to ground floor. Data infrastructure upgrade includes 91 cable runs and 22 new data points.

Commissioning & Hygiene Measures

Final phase includes ductwork cleaning, water sterilisation and comprehensive commissioning to ensure all systems operate reliably and efficiently.

Project Overview

Project Detail

St Peter's Health Centre in Burnley underwent a comprehensive Mechanical, Electrical and Public Health refurbishment to bring ageing building services infrastructure up to modern clinical standards. The project was delivered under the Vital Synergy Subcontract Agreement, covering the full spectrum of building services across a multi-storey healthcare facility.

The scope required careful co-ordination across multiple disciplines, sequenced to maintain operational continuity where possible and minimise disruption to building occupants throughout the works.

Pipework & Mechanical Systems

The Mechanical scope commenced with a full strip-out of all redundant services and infrastructure. New Low Temperature Hot Water pipework was installed throughout, utilising Geberit multi-layer pipework for reliability and longevity. Phenolic insulation was applied to all new runs to comply with current energy efficiency requirements and reduce thermal losses across the distribution network.

Isolation valves were positioned at all key service junctions to facilitate future maintenance access and to allow individual zones to be isolated without affecting wider system operation. All pipework routes were agreed with the principal contractor prior to installation to avoid clashes with other trades working concurrently on site.

Ventilation & Sanitaryware

Mechanical Ventilation with Heat Recovery units were installed in key areas of the building, with associated fire dampers fitted throughout to satisfy fire strategy requirements. The hot and cold-water systems were upgraded to current standards, with new sanitaryware installed across all clinical and support areas, selected to meet the infection control requirements applicable to a primary healthcare environment.

Water systems were designed and installed in accordance with HTM 04-01 guidance, with appropriate zoning and provisions for regular flushing and temperature monitoring to manage Legionella risk throughout the facility.

Electrical & BMS Infrastructure

The Electrical scope involved a complete replacement of power supply containment from the ninth-floor plant area to ground floor level. Ninety-one cable runs were installed to support the updated distribution network, with 22 new data points provided to support operational connectivity requirements. All new electrical infrastructure was integrated with the Building Management System, enabling centralised monitoring and control of the Mechanical and Electrical services.

BMS connectivity was designed to allow future expansion as building usage evolves, providing the facility with a robust and adaptable platform for ongoing energy management and compliance reporting.

Commissioning & Handover

All ductwork was cleaned prior to commissioning, with water systems sterilised in accordance with the relevant Water Regulations and HTM guidance. Full system commissioning was carried out by Vital Synergy's commissioning team, with all results witnessed, documented and submitted as part of the project handover package.

Operation and Maintenance manuals, as-fitted drawings and commissioning certificates were provided to the client on practical completion, ensuring all systems are fully documented and compliant for ongoing management.
