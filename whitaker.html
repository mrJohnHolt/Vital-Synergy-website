Case Study / Cultural & Heritage

The Whitaker Museum

Gas Safety System Upgrade and Compliance
Sector Cultural / Heritage
Rawtenstall, Lancashire
Executive Summary

The Whitaker Museum required a review and rectification of its kitchen gas safety interlock system to ensure full compliance with safety regulations. The system had critical issues including incorrect wiring, a faulty safety push button, and a CO2 monitor not wired to isolate the gas solenoid valve when necessary. Vital Synergy investigated and resolved all system faults within a single visit, restoring compliance and operational reliability with minimal disruption to museum operations.

Scope of Works
Investigation & Assessment

Full site inspection of the kitchen gas interlock system to identify compliance gaps. Assessment confirmed incorrect wiring on the S&S Northern CT1250 gas interlock unit, use of temporary terminal blocks, and a faulty external gas safety push button located near the kitchen doorway.

Rewiring & Component Replacement

The S&S Northern CT1250 gas interlock unit was fully rewired to the correct specification, eliminating temporary terminal blocks throughout. The faulty external gas safety push button was replaced with a compliant unit, restoring manual shut-off capability at the kitchen entrance.

Safety Testing & Verification

Full safety checks conducted on all system components including the gas solenoid valve and fan pressure switches. Pressure switch operations verified to confirm gas shut-off in the absence of airflow. CO2 monitor wiring corrected to ensure effective gas solenoid valve isolation in the event of a carbon monoxide detection event.

Compliance & Recommendations

All works completed in line with current UK gas safety regulations. Expert recommendation provided to terminate fire alarm cabling in the EM safety shut-off rather than the BMS control terminal, enhancing emergency shut-off functionality and improving the system's response to fire events.

Project Overview

Project Detail

The Whitaker is a museum and art gallery in Rawtenstall that plays an important role in the cultural life of the Rossendale Valley. Like many heritage buildings, its operational infrastructure had evolved incrementally over time, and a routine review identified significant compliance deficiencies in the kitchen's gas safety interlock system. Vital Synergy was called to investigate, diagnose, and remediate the issues within a single planned visit to minimise disruption to the museum's day-to-day programme.

The investigation uncovered a combination of wiring errors and failed components that, in combination, meant the system could not perform its primary safety function reliably. Each fault was addressed methodically, with all remediation work completed and verified before the engineer left site.

Investigation & Findings

On arrival, a full inspection of the S&S Northern CT1250 gas interlock unit was carried out. The wiring was found to be incorrect, with temporary terminal blocks present in the circuit that introduced points of potential failure and did not meet the standard required for a safety-critical installation. The external gas safety push button near the kitchen doorway was tested and found to be faulty, meaning that manual shut-off from that location was not operational.

A further critical finding was that the CO2 monitor had not been correctly wired to isolate the gas solenoid valve in the event of an activation. This meant that in a carbon monoxide detection scenario, the system would alert but would not take the required action of isolating the gas supply, representing a significant gap in the safety response chain.

Rewiring & Rectification

The CT1250 interlock unit was fully rewired to the correct specification, with all temporary terminal blocks removed and replaced with permanent, compliant connections. The faulty push button near the kitchen doorway was replaced with a fully functional unit, restoring manual gas shut-off capability at that location. All remedial wiring was completed and labelled in accordance with current standards.

The CO2 monitor wiring was corrected to ensure that an activation event would correctly trigger isolation of the gas solenoid valve. This closes what had been a fundamental gap in the emergency response sequence and ensures the system now behaves as designed in all foreseeable fault conditions.

Safety Verification & Testing

On completion of the remedial works, a full functional test of all system components was carried out. Fan pressure switch operations were verified to confirm that gas shut-off occurs correctly in the absence of adequate airflow. The gas solenoid valve was tested to confirm correct isolation response under each demand condition. All test results were recorded and all components confirmed as fully operational before the engineer left site.

Recommendations for Future-Proofing

In addition to the remedial works, a recommendation was made to terminate the fire alarm cabling at the EM safety shut-off terminal rather than the BMS control terminal. This relatively straightforward change would ensure that a fire alarm activation directly triggers an emergency gas isolation, rather than relying on the BMS to interpret and act on the signal. The recommendation was provided in writing to the museum's facilities team for consideration and scheduling.
