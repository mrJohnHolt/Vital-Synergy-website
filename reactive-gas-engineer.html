Reactive Gas Engineer

The Role

Do you have strong experience maintaining, servicing, and repairing commercial and domestic heating systems? Are you detail-oriented, safety-driven, and committed to delivering fast, reliable solutions that keep customers safe, warm, and satisfied?

Vital Synergy is looking for a skilled and proactive Reactive Gas Engineer to join our growing engineering team. This is more than a traditional gas engineering role, it's an opportunity to support essential commercial environments (including schools), uphold safety and compliance standards, and contribute to the delivery of efficient, future-focused heating solutions.

What You Will Do

Role Overview

This is a full time, permanent field position. The primary responsibility of the Reactive Gas Engineer is to maintain, service, and repair heating and hot water systems across both commercial and domestic properties. The role is heavily focused on commercial environments, particularly schools and similar facilities with the expectation to respond promptly to system faults, ensure safe operation, and deliver high-quality customer service.

You'll represent Vital Synergy ensuring systems run smoothly, safely, and efficiently.

Key Responsibilities

  • Service, maintain, and repair gas systems and appliances in commercial and domestic settings.
  • Diagnose faults and carry out reactive repairs to minimise downtime.
  • Conduct inspections to identify risks, defects, or safety hazards.
  • Repair or replace faulty components and equipment.
  • Test systems for leaks and ensure full compliance with gas safety regulations.
  • Commission boilers and heating systems, including strip-down and reassembly.
  • Work with a range of technologies, including:
    • UFH (Underfloor Heating)
    • ASHP (Air Source Heat Pumps)
    • GSHP (Ground Source Heat Pumps)
  • Provide technical advice and support to clients on system use and maintenance.
  • Collaborate with colleagues to achieve efficient project delivery.
  • Maintain all required Health & Safety documentation (Risk Assessments, Safe Systems of Work, COSHH).

What We Are Looking For

Experience & Qualifications

  • CCN1, CENWAT, HTR1, CKR1 (Commercial qualifications desirable).
  • CSCS card (preferred)
  • Proven experience working on both commercial and domestic gas systems.
  • Strong mechanical aptitude and understanding of heating and hot water systems.
  • Assemble components, and use power tools safely and effectively.
  • Plumbing experience.

  • Familiarity with relevant hand tools and safe working practices.
  • Valid UK driving licence

  • Experience working in school environments or similar commercial settings.
  • Knowledge of renewable heating technologies (ASHP/GSHP – Preferred)
  • Ability to manage reactive workloads independently.
  • Excellent problem-solving, communication, and customer-service skills.
