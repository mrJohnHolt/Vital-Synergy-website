Case Study / Education

Roseacre Primary Academy & Nursery

Comprehensive Heating System Upgrade
Sector Education
Blackpool, Lancashire
Executive Summary

Roseacre Primary Academy & Nursery required a full heating system upgrade to improve energy efficiency, sustainability, and comfort. Vital Synergy designed, installed, and optimised a modern heating infrastructure encompassing full replacement of distribution pipework and heating emitters, air conditioning for specific classrooms, and a comprehensive upgrade of the control system.

Scope of Works
Energy Performance & Feasibility

Detailed assessment of the existing heating infrastructure to identify inefficiencies and establish a strategic plan for optimisation. Site surveys and feasibility studies were carried out alongside compatibility assessments and phased installation planning to minimise disruption to daily school operations.

Control System Upgrade

Upgraded controls with new actuators and localised room controls, new wiring and containment, and zoning configured for different school areas. New air conditioning controls installed for Year 4 and Year 5 classrooms, providing precise room-by-room temperature regulation.

Heating Emitters & Efficiency

High-performance heating emitters installed throughout, including low-level, ceiling-mounted, and high-level fan convectors, alongside radiant panels and LST radiators in pre-school and SEN areas. Advanced phenolic insulation applied to all new pipework with automated controls integrated throughout.

Plant Room & Distribution

Upgraded plant room with new pressurisation units, dirt separation systems, and control valves. Existing boilers retrofitted to work with the new low temperature distribution design. New distribution pipework and high-efficiency fan convectors and radiant panels installed throughout the building.

Project Overview

Project Detail

Roseacre Primary Academy & Nursery in Blackpool presented a familiar challenge for many schools of its age: an outdated heating system that was costly to run, difficult to control, and no longer fit for purpose. High energy consumption, uneven heat distribution across classrooms, and growing non-compliance with current energy efficiency regulations prompted the school to commission a comprehensive upgrade. Vital Synergy was appointed to design, supply, and install a complete modern heating infrastructure with minimal disruption to the school's operational timetable.

The project was delivered in a phased programme, carefully sequenced around the school calendar to ensure classrooms remained in use throughout. Close collaboration with school management at every stage ensured that programme decisions reflected the operational reality of a live educational environment.

Energy Performance Review & Project Development

The project commenced with a full energy performance review of the existing infrastructure. The assessment identified the primary sources of inefficiency across the distribution network, heating emitters, and control systems, and provided the technical basis for a targeted replacement programme. Feasibility studies were carried out to confirm the compatibility of new equipment with the existing building fabric and boiler plant, and a phased installation plan was developed to support continuity of operations throughout the works.

Working alongside school management, Vital Synergy developed a tailored heating solution that addressed both the immediate performance deficiencies and the school's longer-term sustainability objectives. All necessary regulatory approvals were obtained prior to commencement, and financial benefit calculations were prepared to support stakeholder engagement and funding exploration.

Control System & Zoning

A central element of the upgrade was the replacement of the existing control infrastructure. New actuators and localised room controls were installed throughout the building, with new wiring and containment routed to serve each zone. The system was configured to allow independent temperature control across different areas of the school, including separate zoning for teaching areas, corridors, and support spaces.

New air conditioning controls were installed specifically for the Year 4 and Year 5 classrooms, addressing the requirements of those spaces during warmer periods. The integrated control platform provides the school's facilities team with a clear, accessible interface for day-to-day management and energy monitoring.

Heating Emitters & Pipework

The full complement of heating emitters was replaced as part of the works. High-performance fan convectors were installed across teaching and general-purpose areas, with ceiling-mounted and high-level units selected where floor space and aesthetic requirements demanded. Radiant panels and low surface temperature radiators were specified for pre-school and SEN areas, reflecting the particular safety and comfort requirements of those environments.

All new distribution pipework was installed to a low temperature design, reducing system operating temperatures and improving the efficiency of the heat generation plant. Phenolic insulation was applied to all new pipework runs, minimising heat loss throughout the distribution network and contributing to the overall reduction in energy consumption.

Plant Room & Commissioning

The ground floor plant room was upgraded to bring the supporting infrastructure in line with the new distribution system. New pressurisation units, dirt separation systems, and control valves were installed, and the existing boilers were retrofitted to operate efficiently within the new low temperature design. The combined effect of these works materially reduced the risk of system degradation and supported the extended service life of the boiler plant.

On completion, a full commissioning programme was carried out and the system was handed over with an energy monitoring capability that allows the school to track consumption, identify anomalies, and demonstrate compliance with current building regulations. Staff training was provided to ensure the school's facilities team could operate and manage the new controls confidently from day one.

Outcomes

The heating system upgrade at Roseacre Primary Academy & Nursery has transformed the school's energy efficiency and comfort levels. Advanced heating technologies, smart controls, and efficient energy distribution have delivered a future-proof solution tailored to the school's needs. The school is now operating within, and in a number of areas exceeding, the requirements of current energy efficiency standards, with measurable reductions in both energy consumption and maintenance demand anticipated over the long term.
