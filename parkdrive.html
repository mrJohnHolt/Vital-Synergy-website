Project Overview

Project Detail

A full mechanical and electrical package was delivered for this commercial facility under Vital Synergy's Standard Terms & Conditions of Sale. The project scope covered the complete installation of heating distribution, pipework infrastructure, water services, commissioning, and quality documentation, with all works completed and handed over within 10 weeks.

The programme was originally estimated at between eight and twelve weeks. Careful planning, proactive sequencing of trades, and close coordination between the site team and building occupants allowed the project to be completed at the lower end of that estimate, with no compromise to quality or compliance.

Mechanical & Electrical Scope

The mechanical installation encompassed pumps, valves and heating distribution manifolds, all integrated into the existing building systems. Pipework was installed throughout the facility, including vertical risers serving each floor level, with isolation valves positioned at all key service junctions to facilitate future maintenance and zone isolation without disruption to wider building operation.

Thermal insulation was applied to all new pipework runs in accordance with current energy efficiency requirements. The insulation specification was selected to minimise heat loss across the distribution network and extend the working life of the installed system, reducing operational costs over the long term.

Health & Safety Compliance

Comprehensive Risk Assessments and Method Statements (RAMS) were prepared prior to the commencement of works. The RAMS documentation addressed COSHH requirements relating to materials used on site, alongside noise and vibration assessments to manage the impact of mechanical works on building users and surrounding occupants.

All health and safety documentation was reviewed and approved before each work phase commenced. Vital Synergy's site team conducted regular toolbox talks throughout the project, ensuring that all operatives remained fully briefed on the specific hazards present at each stage of the installation.

Water Systems & Commissioning

Hot and cold-water services were installed throughout the facility, with all valves and pipework sized and routed to meet current water regulations. On completion of installation, the water systems were subject to a full chlorination programme to meet the requirements of the relevant Water Regulations guidance prior to handover.

On-site commissioning was carried out by Vital Synergy's commissioning team and encompassed pressure testing of all new pipework, system balancing to ensure correct flow distribution, and a full flushing regime. All commissioning results were witnessed, documented and submitted as part of the formal certification package at practical completion.

Quality Assurance & Documentation

Factory Acceptance Testing was carried out on all plant control panels at the manufacturer's facility before delivery to site. This ensured that all control equipment arrived on site fully tested and ready for integration, reducing the risk of site-based commissioning delays and confirming that panels met the project specification prior to installation.

A complete Operation and Maintenance Manual was compiled and issued to the client at handover. The O&M Manual included full schematics of all installed systems, commissioning and test certification, manufacturer product data sheets, and lifecycle replacement schedules, providing the building owner with a comprehensive reference document for the ongoing management and maintenance of all M&E services.
