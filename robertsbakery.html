Case Study / Industrial

Roberts Bakery

Industrial Ventilation Upgrade
Project Value £53,738 ex VAT
Industrial  ·  Live Production Facility
Executive Summary

A targeted industrial ventilation upgrade delivered within a live production facility, designed to enhance operational efficiency, hygiene and burner performance without interrupting bakery output.

Scope of Works
Ventilation & Hygiene Improvements

Removal and upgrade of outdated ventilation components to enhance operational efficiency and safety. External louvres equipped with insect and vermin mesh installed to improve air intake hygiene and maintain product quality standards throughout the facility.

Infrastructure Upgrades

Upgrade of medium-pressure gas infrastructure and high voltage electrical infrastructure. New automated control systems installed to improve operational efficiencies and enable real-time monitoring of production environment conditions.

Burner & Plenum Works

Fabrication and installation of bespoke airflow plenums designed to stabilise the forced gas burner air supply. Engineered to optimise burner operation and combustion efficiencies, reducing energy waste and improving consistency of the baking process.

Commissioning

Comprehensive commissioning activities carried out including airflow checks, system balancing, verification procedures and thorough documentation of the complete installation, ensuring all works met specification and regulatory requirements.

Project Overview

Project Detail

Vital Synergy was appointed to carry out a targeted ventilation and infrastructure upgrade at Roberts Bakery, a live industrial production facility. The scope of works was carefully sequenced to avoid disruption to ongoing bakery output, with all works planned and executed around the operational demands of the production environment.

The project encompassed ventilation improvements, gas and electrical infrastructure upgrades, bespoke plenum fabrication and full commissioning. All phases were co-ordinated closely with the client's site management team to maintain production continuity and comply with the hygiene and food safety standards applicable to a live food manufacturing environment.

Ventilation & Hygiene Improvements

The existing ventilation components had reached the end of their serviceable life and were contributing to air quality and hygiene concerns within the production environment. All redundant equipment was removed and replaced with upgraded components selected to meet current industrial ventilation standards and the specific demands of a food production facility.

External louvres were fitted with insect and vermin mesh to prevent ingress through air intake points. In a live food manufacturing environment, contamination control at all system boundaries is a fundamental requirement, and the new louvre specifications were selected and installed to provide robust, long-term protection without restricting airflow performance.

Infrastructure Upgrades

The medium-pressure gas infrastructure serving the production area was upgraded to support the revised burner configuration and ensure safe, reliable operation within current gas safety regulations. All gas works were carried out by Gas Safe registered engineers, with full pressure testing and tightness testing completed prior to recommissioning.

High voltage electrical infrastructure was upgraded in parallel with the gas works. New automated control systems were installed to replace manual controls, providing the facility with improved operational efficiencies and the ability to monitor environmental conditions and system performance in real time. The new controls also support predictive maintenance by logging operational data for review by the site team.

Burner & Plenum Works

The forced gas burner serving the production ovens required a stable, consistent air supply to operate efficiently. Variations in inlet airflow were contributing to combustion inconsistencies and energy losses. To resolve this, bespoke airflow plenums were designed and fabricated by Vital Synergy's in-house team, engineered specifically for the geometry and airflow characteristics of the existing installation.

The plenums were installed to provide a stabilised air supply to the burner, eliminating the turbulence that had been affecting combustion quality. Following installation, burner performance was verified against the manufacturer's specification, confirming improved combustion efficiency and a more consistent heat output across the production cycle.

Commissioning & Documentation

All systems were commissioned on completion of the installation works. Airflow checks were carried out across the ventilation network and balanced to achieve the design flow rates at each outlet and inlet point. System verification procedures confirmed that all equipment was operating within the specified parameters before the facility was returned to full production use.

Thorough documentation of the complete installation was compiled and issued to the client at handover. Records include as-installed drawings, commissioning test results, gas tightness certificates, electrical test certificates and equipment data sheets, providing Roberts Bakery with a complete reference for ongoing maintenance and future compliance inspections.
