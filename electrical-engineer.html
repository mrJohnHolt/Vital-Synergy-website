Electrical Engineer

The Role

Are you an experienced Electrical Engineer with a passion for high-quality installations and intelligent system design?

Are you an experienced Electrical Engineer in Lancashire looking to advance your career? Do you want to join a forward thinking company that invests in your growth and offers strong long term progression?

At Vital Synergy, we are looking for self driven and adaptable professionals who take pride in their work and have a sharp eye for detail. As an Electrical Engineer, you will play a key role in designing, installing, and commissioning electrical systems across commercial and domestic environments.

We work across sectors throughout the North West, including education, healthcare, commercial, and leisure facilities. This is a fantastic opportunity for an Electrical Engineer who enjoys technical challenges, takes ownership, and commits to delivering high standards of quality and safety.

The Company

About Vital Synergy

With over 20 years of engineering excellence, Vital Synergy is a trusted partner in delivering end-to-end building solutions. We support the transition to clean energy and low-carbon systems through innovative building services engineering, renewable energy integration, energy efficiency solutions, and collaborative partnerships.

Our Expertise

We specialise in the design, installation, and management of decentralised energy and multi-utility networks, focusing on low and zero carbon technologies. Our expertise spans commercial heating, industrial plumbing, and mechanical engineering, supported by remote access BMS platforms that enable clients to monitor and optimise energy use.

Our Portfolio

Our portfolio includes projects across Commercial, Industrial, Education, Housing, Local Authority, and Agricultural sectors. From Air Source Heat Pumps to high-efficiency commercial plant rooms.

What You Will Do

Role Overview

As an Electrical Engineer, you will be responsible for the design, installation, and commissioning of electrical systems, ensuring all work meets technical, safety, and quality standards across multiple projects.

Key Responsibilities

  • Read and interpret electrical schematics and mechanical drawings
  • Design and plan efficient system layouts
  • Install and commission electrical systems
  • Support site preparation including containment and equipment installation
  • Produce accurate system diagrams and documentation
  • Troubleshoot faults, connectivity issues, and system anomalies
  • Conduct testing and verification to ensure system performance
  • Collaborate with colleagues and clients to meet project requirements
  • Support project delivery, documentation, and case study development

Location and Hours

  • Projects primarily across the North West
  • Occasional travel and overnight stays required
  • Flexibility to work day and twilight shifts
  • Twilight shifts run 4:00 PM to 2:00 AM, four nights per week with Fridays off
